How We Help

Rather than treating symptoms in isolation, we address multiple dimensions of the homeless experience simultaneously.

♨

Public Microwaves

We install small, wall-mounted microwaves in areas of high homelessness. People can heat shop-bought meals for around £3, choosing what they want to eat — restoring dignity and autonomy rather than relying solely on soup kitchens with limited menus.

⚡

The Energy Gym

Our gym uses modified dumbbells, rowing machines and exercise bikes that generate electricity through kinetic energy. The power is stored in batteries and used to run our facilities. Members of the public help the homeless simply by working out.

♥

Donation Centre & Safe Space

Open 24/7 and staffed by volunteers, our centre provides free microwaves, dishwashers, toilets, sleeping bags, blankets and medical consultations with a qualified doctor. A warm, safe place when you need it most.

The Science Behind the Gym

Every workout generates real electricity that powers hot meals and essential facilities for people in need.

1

Kinetic Energy

Gym users pull cables or pedal bikes, producing kinetic energy through physical movement.

2

Generators

Built-in generators convert the kinetic energy into mechanical power.

3

Electricity

Mechanical power is converted into usable electrical energy.

4

Stored & Used

Electricity is stored in batteries and used to power microwaves, dishwashers and facilities.

Our Bournemouth Centre

A purpose-built, fully accessible facility designed to feel welcoming — not institutional.

Ground Floor

The building is divided into two main areas, each serving a vital purpose:

  • Energy Gym — housing the modified exercise machines, battery storage and electrical infrastructure
  • Donation Centre — public microwaves, dishwashers, seating areas and reception

First Floor

  • Free public toilet facilities
  • Two consultation rooms — one general drop-in, one with a qualified doctor

Acquired for £55,356.99 • Fully accessible
