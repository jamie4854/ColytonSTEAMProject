What we do

Three things, done properly.

We don't try to solve everything. We focus on what we can actually deliver.

Public Microwaves

Wall-mounted microwaves in areas of high homelessness. A shop-bought meal costs about £3. People choose what they eat. That matters more than most people realise.

The Energy Gym

Modified dumbbells, rowing machines and exercise bikes that generate electricity through kinetic energy. Members of the public work out, the power goes into batteries, and those batteries run our facilities.

24/7 Safe Space

Staffed by volunteers. Free microwaves, dishwashers, toilets, sleeping bags, blankets, and medical consultations with a qualified doctor. Always open. No questions asked.

The science

How a workout becomes a hot meal

Every rep generates real, usable electricity.

1

Kinetic Energy

Gym users pull cables or pedal bikes, producing kinetic energy through physical movement.

2

Generators

Built-in generators convert the kinetic energy into mechanical power.

3

Electricity

Mechanical power is converted into usable electrical energy.

4

Stored and Used

Electricity is stored in batteries and used to power microwaves, dishwashers and lighting.

The building

Our Bournemouth Centre

Acquired for £55,356.99. Fully accessible. Currently being fitted out.

Ground Floor

The building is split into two main areas:

  • Energy Gym — modified exercise machines, battery storage, and electrical infrastructure
  • Donation Centre — public microwaves, dishwashers, seating areas and reception

First Floor

  • Free public toilet facilities
  • Two consultation rooms — one general drop-in, one with a qualified doctor

The space is a blank canvas right now. Two large industrial units, ready to be converted.

Get involved

There are a few ways you can help.

Volunteer

Staff the reception, help with meal distribution, or support the medical team. Even a few hours a week makes a difference.
